The Owl

Owls hunt mostly small mammals, insects, and other birds, although a few species specialize in hunting fish.

  • scientific name: Strigiformes
  • average length: 13 to 70 cm
  • average life span: 5-12 years
  • habitat: coniferous forests, mountains, deserts, and plains

Owls have round, forward-looking eyes, a sharply hooked beak, and acute hearing and vision. They are 5–28 in. (13–70 cm) long. The feathers of some species form a disk framing the face or ear tufts that help locate prey by reflecting sound to the ears.
